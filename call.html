TRC Call to Action #14

Protecting and Revitalizing Indigenous Languages in Canada

What is Call to Action #14?

Call to Action #14 from the Truth and Reconciliation Commission (TRC) calls on the Government of Canada to recognize Indigenous languages as a fundamental part of Canadian culture and identity.

It also emphasizes the urgent need to preserve, revitalize, and strengthen Indigenous languages that have been impacted by historical government policies.

Why This Matters

Indigenous languages are not just forms of communication — they carry culture, knowledge, traditions, and identity.

Due to residential schools and assimilation policies, many Indigenous languages were disrupted or lost across generations.

This loss has had long-term effects on communities, including weakened cultural transmission and fewer fluent speakers today.

Historical Context

From the late 1800s to the late 1900s, residential schools in Canada forced Indigenous children to speak English or French only.

Speaking Indigenous languages was often punished, leading to widespread language loss.

The Truth and Reconciliation Commission (2015) recognized this harm and issued 94 Calls to Action, including protection of Indigenous languages.

What Is Being Done Today?

In 2019, Canada passed the Indigenous Languages Act, which supports the recovery and revitalization of Indigenous languages.

Today, Indigenous communities, educators, and content creators are using schools, cultural programs, and social media to teach and promote Indigenous languages.

Why It Still Matters Today

Language is deeply connected to identity and belonging. When a language is lost, entire ways of understanding the world can disappear.

Supporting Indigenous languages is an important part of reconciliation in Canada.
