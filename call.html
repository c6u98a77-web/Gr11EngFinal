What Canada Was Asked To Do

Recognize Indigenous languages as fundamental, urgent, and worth protecting.

Call to Action #14 asks the federal government to create an Aboriginal Languages Act based on the idea that Indigenous languages are a valued part of Canadian culture and society, and that there is an urgent need to preserve, revitalize, and strengthen them.

What was harmed?

Residential schools and assimilation policies interrupted language learning across generations. Many children were punished for speaking their languages.

What can disappear with a language?

Stories, songs, humour, land knowledge, ceremony, family memory, and unique ways of understanding the world can become harder to pass on.

Who leads revitalization?

First Nations, Metis, and Inuit communities, Elders, language keepers, educators, families, youth, and digital creators lead the work.

Past Harm

Language Suppression

Indigenous children were often forced to speak English or French only. This created language breaks between Elders, parents, and young people.

Present Action

Community Renewal

Communities are teaching language through schools, family learning, social media, workshops, recordings, and cultural programs.

Reconciliation

Shared Responsibility

Supporting Indigenous languages means listening to Indigenous leadership and respecting language as living cultural knowledge.
